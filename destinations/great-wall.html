Great Wall

The easiest iconic day trip from Beijing, with very different sections depending on your crowd tolerance, fitness, and photo goals.

Best section for you

Mutianyu is the best default for first-time visitors: scenic, restored, less intense than Badaling, and manageable with private transfer or bus. Badaling is the easiest by public transport and has strong accessibility, but it can be crowded. Jinshanling is better for hikers and photographers who want a wilder feel.

How to go

  • Private transfer: simplest for families and tight schedules.
  • Bus/group day tour: cheaper, but check shopping-stop policies.
  • Public transport: possible, but names and station transfers can slow first-timers.

Ticket and timing strategy

Book cable car or shuttle options when available and bring the passport used for booking. Arrive early, avoid Chinese public holidays, and pack water because exposed wall sections get hot and windy.

Good pairings

Pair with the Forbidden City on another day, not the same day unless you are on a very compressed itinerary. For a classic route, see the 10-day first China plan.
